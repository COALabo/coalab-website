## Join Our Lab
<a id="join"></a>

COALAB is always interested in connecting with motivated students and researchers whose interests align with speech-language pathology, language development, reading and writing, cognition, neuroscience, and artificial intelligence.

Although we may not always have advertised openings, we strongly encourage prospective candidates to get in touch to discuss potential research fit and future opportunities.

### How to Get in Touch

Prospective applicants are invited to contact **Dr. Selçuk Güven** by email and include:
- A brief description of research interests
- A CV
- (If applicable) transcripts or writing samples
- Any relevant experience (clinical, computational, experimental, or interdisciplinary)

---

### PhD Students

COALAB supervises PhD students interested in speech-language, reading–writing, and cognition research from:
- Computational
- Cognitive
- Neuroscientific
- Clinically grounded perspectives

Doctoral supervision is offered through multiple academic pathways, depending on the student’s background and objectives. Interested candidates should contact **Dr. Selçuk Güven** directly to discuss research alignment prior to applying.

---

### Master’s Students

Master’s students can join COALAB through:
- Research assistantships  
- Thesis projects  
- Travail dirigé

Students interested in completing a master’s project within the lab are encouraged to reach out with a short description of their interests and academic background.

---

### Undergraduate Research Assistants / Interns

COALAB regularly hosts undergraduate students and interns who wish to gain research experience in areas such as:
- Data collection and annotation
- Behavioral and clinical research
- Computational analysis
- Literature reviews and experimental support

Interested students should contact **Dr. Selçuk Güven** by email with a CV and a short statement of interest.

---

### Visiting Scholars & Collaborators

COALAB welcomes visiting scholars and research collaborators from other institutions. Researchers interested in short-term visits, sabbatical collaborations, or joint projects are invited to contact Dr. Selçuk Güven
